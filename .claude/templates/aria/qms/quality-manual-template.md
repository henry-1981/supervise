## 8. Product Realization

### 8.1 Planning of Product Realization

The organization plans and develops product realization processes.

**Product Realization Planning** includes:

- Determination of product quality objectives and requirements
- Establishment of processes, documents, and resources needed
- Required verification, validation, monitoring, inspection, and test activities
- Product acceptance criteria
- Records needed to provide evidence of conformity

**Planning Output**: Documented plan for each product type

### 8.2 Customer-Related Processes

**Procedure Reference**: QP-7.2 Customer-Related Processes

#### 8.2.1 Determination of Requirements Related to Product

Requirements related to product include:

- Customer-specified requirements
- Requirements not stated by customer but necessary for intended use
- Statutory and regulatory requirements
- Any additional requirements determined by organization

#### 8.2.2 Review of Requirements Related to Product

The organization reviews requirements related to product before commitment to supply.

**Review includes**:

- Product requirements defined
- Contract or order requirements differing from those previously expressed are resolved
- Organization has ability to meet defined requirements

**Review Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.2.2

#### 8.2.3 Customer Communication

The organization determines and implements effective arrangements for communicating with customers:

- Product information
- Inquiries, contracts, or order handling
- Customer feedback, including customer complaints

### 8.3 Design and Development

**Procedure Reference**: QP-7.3 Design and Development

#### 8.3.1 Design and Development Planning

The organization plans and controls design and development.

**Design and development planning** determines:

- Design and development stages
- Review, verification, and validation activities for each stage
- Responsibilities and authorities for design and development
- Interface management between different groups

**Planning Output**: Design and development plan

#### 8.3.2 Design and Development Inputs

Inputs relating to product requirements are:

- Function, performance, and safety requirements
- Applicable statutory and regulatory requirements
- Applicable standards, codes of practice, and guidelines
- Requirements derived from similar previous designs
- Other requirements essential for design and development

**Input Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.3.2

#### 8.3.3 Design and Development Outputs

Design and development outputs are:

- Provided in a form suitable for verification against inputs
- Approved before release

**Outputs include**:

- Specifications and drawings
- Purchasing requirements
- Information for production
- Information for use (IFU)
- Acceptance criteria

#### 8.3.4 Design and Development Review

Systematic reviews of design and development are conducted.

**Review Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.3.4

#### 8.3.5 Design and Development Verification

Verification is performed to ensure outputs meet input requirements.

**Verification Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.3.5

#### 8.3.6 Design and Development Validation

Validation is performed to ensure resulting product is capable of meeting requirements for intended use.

**Validation Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.3.6

#### 8.3.7 Design and Development Changes

Design and development changes are identified, reviewed, controlled, and recorded.

**Change Control**: Per Procedure QP-7.3.7

### 8.4 Purchasing

**Procedure Reference**: QP-7.4 Purchasing

#### 8.4.1 Purchasing Process

The organization ensures purchased product conforms to specified purchasing requirements.

**Supplier Selection**: Based on ability to supply product per requirements

**Supplier Evaluation**: Per Procedure QP-7.4.1

#### 8.4.2 Purchasing Information

Purchasing information describes the product to be purchased, including:

- Requirements for approval of product, procedures, processes, and equipment
- Requirements for qualification of personnel
- Requirements for QMS

**Approval of Purchasing Information**: Before communication to supplier

#### 8.4.3 Verification of Purchased Product

The organization establishes and implements inspection or other activities necessary for ensuring purchased product meets specified purchasing requirements.

**Verification Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.4.3

### 8.5 Production and Service Provision

**Procedure Reference**: QP-7.5 Production and Service Provision

#### 8.5.1 Control of Production and Service Provision

The organization plans and carries out production and service provision under controlled conditions.

**Controlled conditions include**:

- Availability of documented information
- Use of suitable equipment
- Availability and use of monitoring and measuring resources
- Implementation of monitoring and measuring activities
- Implementation of release, delivery, and post-delivery activities

#### 8.5.2 Validation of Processes for Production and Service Provision

The organization validates any processes for production and service provision where resulting output cannot be verified by subsequent monitoring or measurement.

**Validation includes**:

- Defined criteria for review and approval of processes
- Approval of equipment and qualification of personnel
- Use of specific methods and procedures
- Requirements for records
- Revalidation

#### 8.5.3 Identification and Traceability

The organization identifies product status by suitable means throughout product realization.

**Traceability**: The organization controls unique identification of product when traceability is a requirement.

**Identification and Traceability Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.5.3

#### 8.5.4 Customer Property

The organization exercises care with customer property while under organization's control.

**Customer Property**: Customer material, tooling, intellectual property

**Customer Property Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.5.4

#### 8.5.5 Preservation of Product

The organization preserves product during internal processing and delivery to intended destination.

**Preservation includes**:

- Identification
- Handling
- Packaging
- Storage
- Protection

### 8.6 Control of Monitoring and Measuring Equipment

The organization determines monitoring and measurements to make, and monitoring and measuring equipment needed to provide evidence of conformity of product to determined requirements.

**Control Requirements**:

- Calibrated or verified at specified intervals
- Adjusted or re-adjusted as necessary
- Identified to enable calibration status to be determined
- Safeguarded from adjustments that would invalidate measurement result
- Protected from damage and deterioration

**Calibration Records**: Per Procedure QP-7.6

## 9. Measurement, Analysis and Improvement

### 9.1 General

The organization plans and implements monitoring, measurement, analysis, and improvement processes needed to:

- Demonstrate conformity to product requirements
- Ensure conformity of QMS
- Continually improve effectiveness of QMS

**Methods Include**:

- Monitoring and measurement of customer satisfaction
- Internal audit
- Monitoring and measurement of processes
- Monitoring and measurement of product
- Control of nonconforming product
- Data analysis
- Improvement

### 9.2 Monitoring and Measurement

#### 9.2.1 Customer Satisfaction

**Procedure Reference**: QP-8.2.1 Customer Satisfaction

The organization monitors information relating to customer perception as to whether organization has met customer requirements.

**Monitoring Methods**:

- Customer surveys
- Feedback from customers
- Complaint analysis
- Order fulfillment data

#### 9.2.2 Internal Audit

**Procedure Reference**: QP-8.2.2 Internal Audit

The organization conducts internal audits at planned intervals to determine whether QMS:

- Conforms to planned arrangements and requirements of ISO 13485
- Is effectively implemented and maintained

**Audit Program**: Planned, established, implemented, and maintained

**Audit Records**: Per Procedure QP-8.2.2

#### 9.2.3 Monitoring and Measurement of Processes

The organization applies suitable methods for monitoring and, where applicable, measurement of QMS processes.

**Process Monitoring Methods**: [Specify for each process]

**Correction and Corrective Action**: Taken when planned results are not achieved

#### 9.2.4 Monitoring and Measurement of Product

The organization monitors and measures product characteristics to verify that product requirements have been met.

**Acceptance Criteria**: Defined prior to product release

**Records**: Evidence of conformity with acceptance criteria and release authorization

### 9.3 Control of Nonconforming Product

**Procedure Reference**: QP-8.3 Control of Nonconforming Product

The organization ensures product that does not conform to product requirements is identified and controlled to prevent unintended use or delivery.

**Nonconforming Product Control**:

- Actions to eliminate detected nonconformity
- Authorization for use, release, or acceptance under concession
- Action to prevent original intended use

**Nonconforming Product Records**: Per Procedure QP-8.3

### 9.4 Analysis of Data

The organization determines, collects, and analyzes appropriate data to demonstrate suitability and effectiveness of QMS and to evaluate where continual improvement of QMS can be made.

**Data Analysis Includes**:

- Feedback from customers
- Conformity to product requirements
- Characteristics and trends of processes and products
- Opportunities for preventive action
- Suppliers

**Analysis Results**: Used for continual improvement

### 9.5 Improvement

#### 9.5.1 General

The organization continually improves QMS through use of:

- Quality policy
- Quality objectives
- Audit results
- Analysis of data
- Corrective actions
- Management review

#### 9.5.2 Corrective Action

**Procedure Reference**: QP-8.5.2 Corrective Action

The organization takes action to eliminate cause of nonconformities to prevent recurrence.

**Corrective Action Procedure**:

- Review nonconformities (including customer complaints)
- Determine causes of nonconformities
- Evaluate need for action to ensure nonconformities do not recur
- Determine and implement action needed
- Records of results of action taken
- Review of corrective action taken

#### 9.5.3 Preventive Action

**Procedure Reference**: QP-8.5.3 Preventive Action

The organization takes action to eliminate causes of potential nonconformities to prevent occurrence.

**Preventive Action Procedure**:

- Determine potential nonconformities and their causes
- Evaluate need for action to prevent occurrence
- Determine and implement action needed
- Records of results of action taken
- Review of preventive action taken
