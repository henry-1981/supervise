### ISO/IEC Standards Writing Conventions

**ISO/IEC Directives, Part 2**

- **Rules for the Structure and Drafting of International Standards**
  - URL: https://www.iso.org/sites/directives/current/part2/index.xhtml
  - Purpose: Official ISO writing rules
  - Key Sections: Normative language (shall/should/may), requirement formulation, cross-references

**Notable Conventions**:
- "shall" indicates mandatory requirements
- "should" indicates recommendations
- "may" indicates permissions
- "can" indicates possibilities and capabilities

## Medical and Scientific Writing

### American Medical Association (AMA) Manual of Style

**11th Edition (2020)**

- **Chapter 11: Correct and Preferred Usage**
  - Purpose: Medical terminology standards
  - Key Topics: Abbreviations, capitalization, numbers

- **Chapter 20: Study Design and Statistics**
  - Purpose: Statistical reporting standards
  - Key Topics: Confidence intervals, p-values, sample size reporting

**AMA Style Rules for Regulatory Documents**:
- Spell out abbreviations on first use
- Use SI units with conversions
- Report confidence intervals with point estimates
- Avoid promotional language

### Council of Science Editors (CSE) Style

**Scientific Style and Format, 8th Edition**

- **Chapter 9: Technical Reports and Standards**
  - Purpose: Structure for technical reports
  - Key Topics: Abstract writing, results presentation, discussion organization

**CSE Guidelines**:
- Use active voice for methods and results
- Reserve passive voice for background
- Present tense for general truths
- Past tense for specific study results

## Plain Language Resources

### Plain Language Action and Information Network (PLAIN)

**Federal Plain Language Guidelines (2011)**

- **URL**: https://www.plainlanguage.gov/guidelines/
- **Purpose**: US federal government plain language standards
- **Key Sections**:
  - Write for your audience
  - Organize the information
  - Choose your words carefully
  - Be concise

**Application to Regulatory Documents**:
- Instructions for use (patient-facing)
- Patient consent forms
- Public-facing regulatory communications

### FDA Plain Language Initiative

**Plain Language in Regulatory Documents**

- **URL**: https://www.fda.gov/about-fda/plain-writing-fda
- **Purpose**: FDA's plain language requirements
- **Scope**: Patient labeling, public health communications, guidance documents

**FDA Plain Language Standards**:
- Use common, everyday words
- Keep sentences short (average 20 words)
- Use active voice
- Use "you" and other pronouns
- Use lists and tables

## Reference Management

### Citation Style for Regulatory Documents

**Regulation Citations**:
```
[Authority] [Part/Section], [Title], [Version/Date]

Examples:
- 21 CFR 820.30, Design Controls, Current through Dec 2023
- ISO 13485:2016, Section 7.3, Design and Development
- EU MDR 2017/745, Annex I, General Safety and Performance Requirements
```

**Standard Citations**:
```
[Standard Number]:[Year], [Title]

Examples:
- IEC 60601-1:2012+AMD1:2020, Medical electrical equipment
- ISO 14971:2019, Application of risk management to medical devices
```

**Guidance Document Citations**:
```
[Agency], [Document Title], [Publication Date], [URL if online]

Examples:
- FDA, "Use of International Standard ISO 10993-1", June 2020
- MEDDEV 2.7/1 rev 4, "Clinical Evaluation", June 2016
```

### Version Control

All cited regulations and standards must include:
- Version number or publication year
- Amendment status if applicable
- Verification date (when you confirmed it was current)

**Example**:
"This document references ISO 13485:2016. Standard was verified current as of January 2026 via ISO website."
